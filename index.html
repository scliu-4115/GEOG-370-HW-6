Part 2

The ratio that I used for the following three maps was doctorate holders aged 25+ in Arizona divided by the total population aged 25+ in Arizona. I multiplied the ratio by 1000 to get the resulting values over 1. I chose this ratio because I believed that Arizona's 25+ population was the best option to put the number of doctorate holders 25+ by county into proportion. Also, most of the other data options I could have used for the ratio would not have captured the whole sample population, and I did not want to use the total population because it would have included people like children who could not possibly hold a doctorate.

Equal Count

Equal Count does a good job of distinguishing between counties with low proportions of doctorate holders aged 25+, but counties with the largest proportions end up getting lumped together under one bracket even though the actual proportions may differ considerably.

Equal Interval

Equal Interval does a good job of distinguishing between counties with high proportions of doctorate holders aged 25+, but counties with the lowest proportions end up getting lumped together under one bracket despite there being a proportionately broad range of numbers covered by that bracket.

Fixed Interval

Fixed Interval splits the difference between Equal Count and Equal Interval. Counties with higher proportions of doctorate holders aged 25+ are well-distinguished and while the counties with the lowest proportions of doctorate holders aged 25+ tend to get lumped under one bracket, that bracket covers a narrower range of numbers than Equal Interval does.
